What do the different weapon types do?
Light Weapons
There are two types : 
Melta Gun :- slower-firing, high-damage , armor-piercing.
Stubber :- faster-firing, lower-damage.
Functions
Melta Gun :- effective against everything that isn't infantry. It will kill infantry fine, but it is slower-firing so you have to be very precise : wasted shots=overheat before entire clump of enemies is killed, if you're not careful.

With both, you want to fire in short, targeted bursts to avoid overheating.

Heavy Weapons
There are three types :
Battle Cannon :- low damage, but very large blast radius(700-1100), around 6-7 round capacity, and slow reload(37-39 sec).
Gattling Cannon :- high damage, lower capacity(4 rounds called "drums"), and faster reload(20-23 sec).
Thermal Cannon :- highest damage, a smaller blast radius(400-700), 6-round capacity, and the slowest reload time(43-47 sec).
Functions
Battle Cannon :- least effective against large enemies, but its large blast radius will take out groups of infantry.
Thermal Cannon :- best against large armoured enemies. It has a small blast radius so you can take out half-clumps of infantry in a pinch, but it's not as good as the Battle Cannon.
Gattling Cannon :- no blast radius, but it reloads quickly. That's the most that can be said for it.

Remember, you can fire a Heavy Weapon multiple times in quick succession if you really need to pound a large enemy quickly.
Mounted Weapons
There are three types :
Autocannon : highest ammo capacity(21-23 rounds), low damage(100%), and shortest reload time(36-38 secs).
Rocket Launcher : medium capacity(15 rounds), medium damage(195%), and long reload time(48-50 secs).
Missile Launcher : low capacity(8 rounds), high damage(296%), and slightly less-long reload time(45-46 secs).

For all three, lesser enemies on screen=lesser rounds fired=less time to reload(it only has to reload the used rounds).

You always want to use the Autocannon because if you're using either of the other weapons and their are more enemies onscreen than available rounds, some of the enemies will not be targeted, while others may be overkilled, thus wasting some of your available damage output.

Recommended Loadout 
This is a matter of individual playstyle, but the best loadout I've found is to use a Melta Gun, Battle Cannon and Autocannon.
Use the Melta Gun to destroy all non-infantry enemies, and use the Battle Cannon to destroy large clumps of infantry. Use the Autocannon to clear things out when tons of enemies appear at once and you get overwhelmed.

The Forge
Using higher-quality/higher-level items in the forge does not always yield higher-level items.
Do not rely on the auto-fill button !
Using lower-level items in the forge tends to yield higher-level gear, so make sure you experiment.

Score Multipliers
Most important thing==keeping up your Kill Multiplier.
The key to doing this is ABD : Always Be Destroying.
Let enemies accumulate onscreen or delay killing an enemy for a bit until the next one is about to appear so that you minimize between killing-delays.
When there are no enemies in sight ; blow up destructible objects(huge statues, huge square pillars, red generators, red barrels, piles of grey ammo boxes, defensive barriers, lighter-coloured pipes, vertical-shaped yellow lights, yellow lanterns, yellow hanging lights, white cylindrical lights, etc.)
